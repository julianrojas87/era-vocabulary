Operational Pointc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://era.europa.eu/ns#OperationalPoint

An operational point (OP) means any location for train service operations, where train services may begin and end or change route, and where passenger or freight services may be provided; operational point also means any location at boundaries between Member States or infrastructure managers.
Is defined by
http://era.europa.eu/ns#
Term status
stable
has super-classes
SpatialThing c, feature
is in domain of
TAF/TAP code dp, operational point type op, unique operational point id dp

pointc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://www.w3.org/2003/01/geo/wgs84_pos#Point

Uniquely identified by lat/long/alt. i.e. spaciallyIntersects(P1, P2) :- lat(P1, LAT), long(P1, LONG), alt(P1, ALT), lat(P2, LAT), long(P2, LONG), alt(P2, ALT). sameThing(P1, P2) :- type(P1, Point), type(P2, Point), spaciallyIntersects(P1, P2).
has super-classes
SpatialThing c
has sub-classes
Node Port c

SpatialThingc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://www.w3.org/2003/01/geo/wgs84_pos#SpatialThing

Anything with spatial extent, i.e. size, shape, or position. e.g. people, places, bowling balls, as well as abstract areas like cubes.
has sub-classes

is in domain of
altitude, latitude, longitude
is in range of
location op
